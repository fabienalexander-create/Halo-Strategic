About

Halo sits outside every department, so someone finally does.

Not another agency. A commercial growth consultancy built on one belief: leadership teams don't need more activity, they need clarity about what to do next.

Why Halo exists

Most businesses don't have a marketing problem.

They have a commercial alignment problem. Marketing, sales, operations, and leadership can each be doing their job well, individually, and the business can still underperform, because nobody is responsible for how the parts fit together. A sales team optimising for closed deals this month and a marketing team optimising for lead volume this quarter can both be doing exactly what they were asked to do, and still be quietly working against each other. Nobody chose the misalignment. It accumulated.

Halo occupies the seat outside every department, asking the question none of them is incentivised to ask: does this actually add up?

The commercial philosophy

Data. Signal. Judgement. Decision. Outcome.

That's the whole of what Halo does, seen from every angle in this site. Data on its own isn't useful. Compressed into a signal, it becomes readable. But the signal is never the decision, judgement is the step that turns a signal into a choice someone actually owns. Halo's job is to make that chain fast, evidence-backed, and trustworthy, not to replace the judgement at the end of it with a dashboard.

Marketing vs. commercial growth

Marketing tells you what happened. Halo tells you what to do about it.

A marketing report tells you the campaign's cost per lead moved. It doesn't tell you whether the real constraint on growth is upstream of marketing entirely, in a sales process that lets leads go cold, or downstream, in a retention gap that means every new customer replaces one who just left. Halo starts by finding out which one it actually is, before recommending anything.

Why Halo isn't another agency

We don't get paid more the more we do.

An agency's incentive is usually more activity: more campaigns, more spend, more retainer hours. Halo's Commercial Audit is a fixed fee, and every recommendation that follows is designed to leave your business more capable of deciding well, not just busier. That's why Halo will tell a client to spend less, wait, or walk away from a tactic that would have billed more. A recommendation that wins the quarter and costs the relationship's trust isn't a result, it's a broken promise with good short-term optics.

Diagnose before prescribing. Tell the truth even when it costs the engagement. Never claim more than the evidence supports. Those aren't slogans, they're the actual rules Halo's own methodology is built to enforce.

Principles

Five things Halo won't compromise on.

Clarity

If a reader needs a follow-up question, the sentence wasn't finished.

Evidence

An opinion needs a number, a test, or a precedent before it becomes a recommendation.

Restraint

Consistency and quiet discipline beat cleverness shipped late or overstated.

Honesty

Advising against work that isn't in the client's interest, even when it costs the engagement.

Who Halo works best with

Real commercial complexity, not a single-channel business.

Halo is built for businesses with more than one department whose decisions affect the others, and leadership teams who want an evidence-backed answer to "what do we do next," not a vendor who wants approval to keep running the same activity.

Halo is honest about who it isn't for, too: businesses looking for the cheapest bid on a defined deliverable, or anyone wanting execution without diagnosis. Both are reasonable things to want. They're just not what Halo does.
