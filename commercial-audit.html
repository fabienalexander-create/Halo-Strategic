The Commercial Audit.

A fixed-fee, evidence-based diagnosis of the one constraint currently limiting your business's profitable growth, not a marketing audit, and not a guess.

Start with a Commercial Diagnostic
What it is

Not a report card. A search for the constraint.

Most audits score everything evenly and hand you a long list. The Commercial Audit is built on a different premise: a business is limited by its weakest link, not the average of all its parts. Rather than scoring every function equally, the Audit is designed to find the one thing, usually one, sometimes two, that's actually setting the ceiling on your growth right now, and to rank every recommendation by how much it moves that specific constraint.

Who it's for

Businesses with real commercial complexity.

More than one department whose decisions affect the others. Leadership teams who want an evidence-backed answer to what to do next, not a vendor asking for approval to keep running the same activity. If you've already decided the tactic and just want someone to build it, the Audit isn't the right starting point, an Implementation engagement might be.

Commercial Leaks

Where revenue actually goes missing.

Most revenue doesn't disappear in one dramatic failure. It leaks through a dozen small, unowned handoffs between departments, a slow response to a new lead, a CRM nobody trusts enough to use properly, a reporting gap that means two departments are arguing over two different numbers. The Audit is built to find and name these specifically, not generically.

FAQ

What people ask before starting.

How much does it cost?

A fixed fee of £3,000 for the Commercial Audit. There's no obligation to proceed to implementation afterwards.

What if the Audit doesn't find a major constraint?

Then we tell you that. The Audit is built to follow the evidence, not to justify its own fee by inventing a problem.

Is this confidential?

Yes. Everything shared during the Audit is treated as confidential and is never disclosed without your permission.

Can this be delivered remotely?

Yes, the Audit is designed to run entirely remotely, including stakeholder interviews and the final presentation.

What happens after the Audit?

If the evidence justifies it, you'll receive a separate Implementation Proposal. If not, you keep the Audit and its recommendations either way.
